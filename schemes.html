Explore Unemployment Assistance Schemes

Get to know the different programs available to support unemployed citizens across India.

Atal Beemit Vyakti Kalyan Yojana (ABVKY)

This scheme by ESIC provides financial support to insured persons who lose their employment involuntarily. Beneficiaries receive up to 50% of average daily wages for 90 days.

Eligibility:
  • Registered under ESIC with valid IP number
  • Completed at least 2 years of insurable employment
  • Paid contributions for a minimum of 78 days in the relevant contribution period
  • Involuntary job loss (not due to resignation)

MNREGA (Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act)

Provides 100 days of guaranteed wage employment per financial year to rural households willing to do unskilled manual work.

Eligibility:
  • Resident of a rural area
  • Household registered under the local Gram Panchayat
  • Willingness to do manual work

PMKVY (Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana)

Provides free skill training and monetary rewards upon certification in various sectors.

Eligibility:
  • Indian citizen aged between 15-45 years
  • Unemployed or school/college dropout
  • Willingness to enroll in certified training centers

State-Level Unemployment Allowance Schemes

Several states provide monthly allowances to educated unemployed youth. Criteria differ across states.

Example (Bihar):
  • Resident of Bihar
  • Age 20–25 years
  • Minimum education qualification: Intermediate (10+2)
  • Registered with local employment exchange

Skill India Mission

A nationwide initiative to train over 40 crore people in different skills by 2025.

Eligibility:
  • Indian citizen aged 15 years or older
  • Unemployed youth, school dropouts, or working individuals seeking certification
  • Interest in recognized skill programs

Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY)

Targets rural youth with comprehensive training and guaranteed placement support.

Eligibility:
  • Aged 15–35 years (up to 45 for vulnerable groups)
  • Belong to a poor rural household
  • Identified through the Social Economic and Caste Census (SECC)
